Phase I: Final Design Review & Audit

Today marks the completion of the theoretical and methodological preparation phase. Groups will present their final design, incorporating feedback from Session 6's Audit. The goal is to get the green light for data collection.

Your Task: Final Design Presentation (10-15 Minutes)

Groups presenting today will cover the four key elements of their design:

  1. Foundation: Final Research Question and Hypotheses.
  2. The Instrument: Operationalization & measurement (The core of your survey/codebook).
  3. Feasibility: Sampling strategy and recruitment plan.
  4. The Ask: Your one remaining, biggest challenge/risk.

Phase II: The Go-Live Checklist

For the groups now moving into data collection, this checklist is mandatory. Rushing the launch can cost you weeks of wasted work on a flawed dataset. Adhering to these rules is non-negotiable for a valid project.

Mission Briefing: Data Collection Launch

The time for planning is over. Your primary mission for the next two weeks is to collect your data and prepare for the Analysis Phase.

  1. Launch Data Collection: Implement your recruitment strategy immediately. Distribute your survey link or begin systematically applying your finalized codebook to your full sample.
  2. Monitor & Document: Keep a running log of your process. Document: the date you launched, where you posted the link, how many participants completed the survey, or how many units have been coded.
  3. Prepare for Analysis: Ensure you have the software (SPSS, R, Excel, Nvivo, etc.) needed for your analysis ready for the next session.
  4. Contingency Plan: If you are struggling to collect data (low response rate, technical issues), formulate a concise plan B (e.g., extend deadline, change recruitment channel, or reduce sample size) to discuss with the lecturer.

The next few sessions (Sessions 8-10) will focus on the practical application of methodology: Survey Techniques, Content Analysis, and Pre-testing. Bring your questions from the field!
